Stock

Overview

Welcome to the Stock Management module of XpertERP, your customized solution for Alkhidmat Foundation Pakistan. This crucial module is designed to give AKFP a clear, real-time view of all our physical assets, including both Inventory (consumables, aid items, program supplies) and Fixed Assets (equipment, vehicles, furniture). By centralizing management of these resources, XpertERP helps us efficiently track what we have, where it is, and how it's being utilized, ensuring optimal resource allocation and accountability across all our humanitarian efforts. This guide will cover the most common tasks within both Inventory and Fixed Assets, complemented by short training videos for easy understanding.

Inventory Management

This section covers the management of consumable items, aid materials, and other goods that are regularly issued or distributed.

Receiving Inventory (Goods Receipt)

When new stock or donations arrive, it's essential to record them accurately in the system.

  • Process:
    1. Navigate to Stock > Stock Entry.
    2. Click "+ Add Stock Entry".
    3. Select "Purpose" as "Material Receipt".
    4. Choose the appropriate "From Warehouse" (if applicable, e.g., "Supplier" or "Donor Virtual Warehouse") and "To Warehouse" (e.g., "Main Warehouse - Islamabad").
    5. In the "Items" table, add each item received:
      • Search and select the "Item Code" (e.g., "Rice - Basmati 5kg").
      • Enter the "Quantity" received.
      • (Optional) Add "Batch No." (for items with expiry dates or specific production lots) and "Expiry Date" if applicable.
    6. Click "Save" then "Submit".
    • Tip: Always ensure the physical count matches the system entry. For donations, use a relevant naming convention or link to a Donation record if applicable.

Video: Short video demonstrating adding a new stock entry for material receipt, showing selection of purpose, warehouses, and adding two different items with quantities.

Issuing Inventory (Goods Issue)

When inventory is distributed to programs, projects, or other departments, it must be issued from the system.

  • Process:
    1. Navigate to Stock > Stock Entry.
    2. Click "+ Add Stock Entry".
    3. Select "Purpose" as "Material Issue".
    4. Choose the "From Warehouse" (where stock is coming from, e.g., "Main Warehouse - Islamabad") and "To Warehouse" (e.g., "Project A - Lahore Warehouse" or "Internal Consumption").
    5. In the "Items" table, add each item being issued:
      • Search and select the "Item Code".
      • Enter the "Quantity" issued.
      • (Optional) If using batches, select the specific "Batch No."
    6. Click "Save" then "Submit".
    • Tip: Ensure you have the necessary approvals (e.g., Project Manager's request) before issuing stock.

Video: Short video showing creation of a material issue stock entry, selecting a source and destination warehouse, and issuing specific items.

Viewing Current Stock Levels

Easily check how much of an item is currently in your warehouses.

  • Process:
    1. Navigate to Stock > Stock Balance.
    2. You can filter by "Item Code", "Warehouse", or "Item Group" to narrow down your search.
    3. The report will display the "Current Quantity" for each item.
  • Alternative: From an "Item" record (Stock > Item), you can also see its "Stock Balance" in various warehouses.

Video: Short video demonstrating filtering the Stock Balance report by item or warehouse to see quantities.

Stock Reconciliation (Addressing Discrepancies)

If the physical count of stock does not match the system's record, a reconciliation entry is needed. Note: This task is typically performed by authorized Inventory/Warehouse Managers.

  • Process:
    1. Conduct a physical count of the items.
    2. Navigate to Stock > Stock Reconciliation.
    3. Click "+ Add Stock Reconciliation".
    4. Select the "Warehouse" where the discrepancy occurred.
    5. In the "Items" table, add the item(s) in question.
    6. Enter the "Current Quantity" (the physical count) and the system will show the "Difference."
    7. Provide a "Reason for Reconciliation" (e.g., "Loss," "Damage," "Incorrect previous entry").
    8. Click "Save" then "Submit". This will adjust the system's stock level to match the physical count.

Fixed Assets Management

This section details how to manage AKFP's long-term assets such as vehicles, machinery, office equipment, and furniture.

Creating a New Fixed Asset Record

When AKFP acquires a new fixed asset, it must be recorded in the system.

  • Process:
    1. Navigate to Assets > Asset.
    2. Click "+ Add Asset".
    3. Enter essential details:
      • "Asset Name" (e.g., "Toyota Hilux - IDH-123").
      • "Asset Category" (e.g., "Vehicles," "IT Equipment," "Furniture").
      • "Purchase Date" and "Purchase Value."
      • "Location" (e.g., "Islamabad Office," "Lahore Orphanage").
      • Assign an "Asset Owner" (the department or individual responsible for it).
      • (Optional) "Asset ID" if you have a physical tag.
    4. Click "Save" then "Submit".
    • Tip: Ensure all relevant purchase documents are kept on file and potentially linked to the asset record.

Video: Short video demonstrating adding a new asset, filling in key details like name, category, purchase value, and location.

Assigning and Transferring Assets

Track who is responsible for an asset and its physical location.

  • Assigning an Asset: When creating a new asset or if it's not assigned, use the "Asset Owner" field on the Asset record.
  • Transferring an Asset:
    1. Navigate to Assets > Asset Transfer.
    2. Click "+ Add Asset Transfer".
    3. Select the "Asset" being transferred.
    4. Choose the "From Employee/Location" and "To Employee/Location."
    5. Enter the "Transfer Date" and a "Reason for Transfer."
    6. Click "Save" then "Submit".

Video: Short video briefly showing how to assign an owner directly on the asset record, then a quick demonstration of creating an Asset Transfer to move an asset from one location to another.

Recording Asset Maintenance

Keep a record of all maintenance performed on assets.

  • Process:
    1. Navigate to Assets > Maintenance Log.
    2. Click "+ Add Maintenance Log".
    3. Select the "Asset" that was maintained.
    4. Enter "Maintenance Date" and "Maintenance Type" (e.g., "Routine Service," "Repair").
    5. Provide a "Description of Work" and "Cost of Maintenance."
    6. Click "Save" then "Submit".

Video: Short video demonstrating adding a simple maintenance log entry for an asset.

Disposing of an Asset

When an asset is no longer usable or sold, it must be disposed of in the system. Note: This requires specific authorization.

  • Process:
    1. Navigate to Assets > Asset.
    2. Open the specific "Asset" record you wish to dispose of.
    3. (Depending on customization) You might find a "Dispose" button or a workflow action. If not, contact your supervisor for the correct procedure.
    4. Enter the "Disposal Date" and "Reason for Disposal" (e.g., "Scrapped," "Sold," "Damaged Beyond Repair").
    5. (If sold) Enter "Selling Price."
    6. Click "Save" then "Submit".

Video: Short video showing opening an asset and initiating a disposal process (briefly, as it's less frequent).

Reports (Inventory & Fixed Assets)

XpertERP provides various reports for comprehensive overview and analysis of your stock and assets.

  • Accessing Reports:
    1. Navigate to Stock > Reports or Assets > Reports.
    2. Select the desired report from the list:
      • Inventory: "Stock Balance," "Stock Ledger," "Item-wise Stock Report," "Batch-wise Stock Report."
      • Fixed Assets: "Asset Register," "Asset Depreciation Schedule," "Asset Maintenance Log Report."
    3. Apply relevant filters (e.g., by Warehouse, Item, Asset Category, Date Range) and click "Refresh" to generate.
