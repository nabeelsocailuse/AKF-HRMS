Accounting & Finance

Overview

Welcome to the Accounts Management module of XpertERP, your customized solution for Alkhidmat Foundation Pakistan. This module is the financial backbone of AKFP, meticulously designed to manage all financial transactions, track expenses, record revenues (including donations), and generate crucial financial reports. By providing a centralized and transparent platform for all accounting activities, XpertERP ensures accurate financial stewardship, enables informed decision-making, and maintains accountability to our donors and stakeholders. This guide will walk you through the most common financial tasks, complemented by short training videos for clear, practical understanding.

Accessing the Accounts Module

To access the Accounts module in XpertERP:

  1. Log In: Open your web browser and navigate to the XpertERP login page (e.g., https://your-akfp-xperterp-url.com).
  2. Enter Credentials: Input your assigned Username and Password.
  3. Dashboard: After successful login, you will land on your XpertERP Dashboard.
  4. Navigate to Accounts:
    • Click on the "Modules" icon (usually a grid or squares) in the top-left corner.
    • Select "Accounts" from the list of modules.
    • Alternatively, use the Search Bar at the top by typing "Accounts" and selecting "Accounts."

Key Features & Common Tasks

The XpertERP Accounts module supports a wide range of financial operations. Here are some of the key areas you'll interact with:

Managing Accounts Payable (Vendor Bills / Expenses)

This section focuses on recording expenses and managing payments to vendors and service providers.

  • Recording a Vendor Bill:
    1. Navigate to Accounts > Purchase Invoice.
    2. Click "+ Add Purchase Invoice".
    3. Select the "Supplier" (Vendor).
    4. Enter the "Invoice No." from the vendor's bill and the "Posting Date."
    5. In the "Items" or "Accounting Dimensions" table:
      • Add the service or item details (e.g., "Electricity Bill," "Stationery").
      • Enter the "Amount."
      • Ensure the correct "Expense Account" is selected (e.g., "Utilities Expenses," "Office Supplies Expense").
      • Crucially, select the appropriate "Cost Centre" or "Project" (e.g., "Orphan Care Project," "Islamabad Office Operations") to allocate the expense.
    6. Click "Save" then "Submit."
  • Making Payments to Vendors:
    1. After submitting a Purchase Invoice, you can directly click "Make Payment" from the invoice itself.
    2. Alternatively, navigate to Accounts > Payment Entry.
    3. Select "Payment Type" as "Pay," choose the "Party Type" as "Supplier" and select the specific "Supplier."
    4. Select the "Mode of Payment" (e.g., "Bank," "Cash").
    5. Enter the "Paid Amount."
    6. Link to the relevant "Purchase Invoice(s)" by selecting them in the "References" table.
    7. Click "Save" then "Submit."

Recording Donations / Accounts Receivable

This section deals with recording incoming funds, primarily donations for AKFP.

  • Recording a Donation (Sales Invoice / Payment Entry):
    • Direct Payment: For cash or bank donations not tied to a formal invoice, navigate to Accounts > Payment Entry.
      1. Select "Payment Type" as "Receive."
      2. Choose "Party Type" as "Customer" (or "Donor" if customized). Select the "Donor Name."
      3. Select "Mode of Payment" (e.g., "Cash," "Bank - HBL").
      4. Enter the "Received Amount."
      5. Ensure the correct "Income Account" is selected (e.g., "Donation Revenue").
      6. Crucially, select the appropriate "Cost Centre" or "Project" to allocate the donation (e.g., "Zakat Fund," "Flood Relief Project").
      7. Click "Save" then "Submit."
    • Sales Invoice (for invoiced services/goods, if applicable): If AKFP issues invoices for services or goods, use Accounts > Sales Invoice to record the receivable, and then Accounts > Payment Entry to record the receipt against that invoice.

General Journal Entries

Journal Entries are used for recording financial transactions that don't fit into standard forms (like purchase invoices or payments), or for adjustments. Note: This is primarily for authorized Finance personnel.

  • Process:
    1. Navigate to Accounts > Journal Entry.
    2. Click "+ Add Journal Entry".
    3. Select the "Posting Date."
    4. In the "Accounts" table, enter the "Account" (e.g., "Cash," "Bank," "Salaries Payable").
    5. Enter the "Debit" or "Credit" amount for each account. Ensure total debits equal total credits.
    6. Add a concise "Remark" explaining the transaction.
    7. Crucially, ensure proper "Cost Centre" or "Project" allocation for relevant accounts.
    8. Click "Save" then "Submit."

Chart of Accounts

The Chart of Accounts (CoA) is the organized list of all financial accounts (assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses) used by AKFP.

  • Viewing the Chart of Accounts:
    1. Navigate to Accounts > Chart of Accounts.
    2. This provides a tree-view structure of all accounts. You can expand and collapse categories to see sub-accounts.
    • Tip: Familiarize yourself with the CoA to understand how different transactions are categorized. Changes to the CoA are restricted to authorized finance administrators.

Video: Short video showing navigation to the Chart of Accounts and briefly scrolling through the tree view.

Reports

The Accounts module generates vital financial reports for management and compliance.

  • Accessing Reports:
    1. Navigate to Accounts > Reports.
    2. Select the desired report from the list:
      • Financial Statements: "Balance Sheet," "Profit and Loss Statement (Income Statement)," "Cash Flow Statement."
      • Operational Reports: "Trial Balance," "General Ledger," "Accounts Payable Summary," "Accounts Receivable Summary," "Budget vs. Actual Report."
    3. Apply relevant filters (e.g., by Period, Company, Cost Centre, Project) and click "Generate Report" or "Refresh" to view.
